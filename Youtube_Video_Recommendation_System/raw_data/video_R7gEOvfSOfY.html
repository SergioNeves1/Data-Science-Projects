Published on Feb 14, 2020

Ellie Ott, Rees Centre, University of Oxford, and David Leslie, Public policy programme, The Alan Turing Institute, present the research and findings of the project Ethics review of machine learning in children’s social care, commissioned by What Works for Children’s Social Care. This report seeks to answer the question “Is it ethical to use machine learning approaches in children’s social care systems and if so, how and under what circumstances?”. In this presentation, you can hear the main findings of the report and the recommendations for the future on how machine learning could be used in children’s social care in a manner that improves outcomes for families and children.

The report is available at: https://whatworks-csc.org.uk/research...
